Bashiru Ramoni

Partner, SimmonsCooper Partners

  • +234 8177440900
  • V-card

He leads our Strategic Business Units which encompasses Commercial and Civil Litigation, Special Tribunal, and Alternative Dispute Resolution practice areas at SimmonsCooper Partners.

Bashiru serves as a Partner at SimmonsCooper. He is a Notary Public and a licensed tax practitioner in Nigeria. Bashiru's extensive experience and in-depth knowledge are evident in his practice areas, which include Commercial & Civil Litigation, Special Tribunals, Public Finance, and Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR). His versatility allows him to provide innovative legal solutions across various domains.

Bashir's dedication to legal scholarship is evident through his insightful contributions to the taxation field. He has published several legal resources on various platforms, showcasing his expertise and commitment to knowledge dissemination. Some of his notable works include:
• "The Excise Duty (Non-Alcoholic, Carbonated and Sweetened Beverages), Regulations 2022: Fundamental Things To Know" published in Mondaq on 6th March, 2023.
• "Capital Gains Tax In The Nigerian Capital Market And Stocks Investment Under Finance Act 2021" published on Mondaq on 2nd March, 2022
• "A Review Of The CBN Guidelines On The Introduction Of E-Evaluator, E-Invoicing For Import And Export In Nigeria" published on Mondaq.
• "The Finance Act 2020 — A Change In The Tax Landscape In Nigeria" published on Mondaq.
• "Branding Value Added Tax & Sales tax as Double Taxation: A Critical Review of A.G. Lagos State v Eko Hotels Ltd & Anor," being Chapter 23 of the book titled: "Value Added tax in Nigeria," to be published by the CITN.
• "Income Taxes: Double Edge Treatment of Interest," New Telegraph, November 4th, 2019; P.14.

His professional, business and community credentials are not limited to his role in SimmonsCooper but includes:
• Member of the Nigerian Bar Association,
• Fellow, Chartered Institute of Taxation in Nigeria (FCTI) and regularly speaks at industry events and conferences.
• Associate member, Business Recovery and Insolvency Practitioners Association of Nigeria (ABR).


Background
Bashiru obtained a Bachelor of Law Degree in Law from University of Ilorin, Kwara and was admitted into the Nigerian Bar in………... He holds a Master of Law degree in University of Ibadan. He later joined SimmonsCooper Partners in 2007 and was appointed a Partner in…… In addition to his role as Partner, he leads the Strategic Business Units that encompasses Commercial and Civil Litigation, Special Tribunal, and Alternative Dispute Resolution practice areas. Bashiru leads the Dispute Resolution team of the firm and is a member of the Management Committee.


Experience & expertise

Selected Matters

Some of Bashir’s key projects and contributions include:

• Lead representation for an oil and gas company in a $9.320 million international commercial transaction dispute involving letter of credit enforcement across multiple countries including Ireland, China, Canada, and Nigeria.

• Tax advisory and consultation for an upstream oil and gas firm, resolving a substantial N1.9 billion tax liability with the Federal Inland Revenue Service.

• Consulted with Oyo State Internal Revenue Service (OYIRS) and Lagos State Internal Revenue Service (LIRS) on increasing Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) through Stamp Duties.

• • Engaged in appellate-level defence for election petition cases in recent general elections across various states including Akwa-Ibom, Benue, Edo, Ebonyi, Jigawa, Kwara, Lagos, Ogun, and Osun.
